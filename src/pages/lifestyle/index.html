Sport and academic performance

We compared extracurricular activites to academic grade performance average of participants in that activity

Overall, people who engaged in performing arts, non-ball sports, and no hobbies had the highest grade averages respectively (excluding non-miscellaneous hobbies), with the average grade ranges from 81.67%, 81.67%, and then 77.5%. Although the difference can be neglible and minor especially with a smaller sample size, there is still some evidence that extracurricular activities can boost academic performance.

According to the United States Department of Education, students engaging in extracurricular activities had higher test scores compared to their peers. It is important to note that there was neglible grade difference between those who engaged in zero, and only one extracurricular activity, which might have been demonstrated by our survey. For males, athletic participation was associated with a higher grade point average; and sport team participation was associated with a higher grade point average for females.

The study also associated extracurricular activities with a more positive mood and attraction to school than non-participants, which was somewhat supported by our survey results.

The grade field worked in ranges and therefore does not produce any more of a precise answer, furthermore we did not survey study habits.

Does a good lifestyle actually help?

We drew connections between sleep and mental health..

The Australian Institute of Family Studies recommends 8-10 hours of sleep for teenagers every night.

Most respondents do not get enough sleep, on an average of 6.87 hours and past 10pm about 5 times per week, which is well below the healthy amount of sleep of 8-10 hours. Furthermore, the average mood reported was 6.6 on the 1-10 scale. While not extremely low, it indicates room for improvement in the surveyed population and a possible correlation between sleep and mood.

The majority of respondents (51.28%) indicated their grades in the 71%-80% range, and 30.77% in the 80-90% range.
The distribution shows high academic performance, however may be skewed as respondents attend a selective school, and were traditionally high performing students.

For those who met the 8 hour minimum recommendation, the grade average was 86%. The average for those who did not meet the recommendation (29 respondents) had an average grade of 82%.
